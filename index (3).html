Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio
Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)
Compares your recent training load (acute, 7-day EWMA) to your long-term load (chronic, 28-day EWMA). A ratio of 0.8–1.3 is the optimal training zone. Below 0.8 indicates undertraining. Above 1.3 signals caution; above 1.5 is high injury risk. Based on Murray et al. 2017 (BJSM).

Monotony Over Time
Monotony (Foster et al.)
Each bar represents one training day, with monotony calculated from a rolling 7-day window. Mean daily load ÷ standard deviation across those 7 days. Measures how varied training is day-to-day. Below 1.0: very high variability, low consistency. 1.0–1.5: good training variation. Above 2.0: athlete is doing the same load every day — a known overtraining risk factor independent of total volume.
Grey <1.0 Low · Green 1.0–1.5 Good · Yellow 1.5–2.0 Monitor · Red >2.0 High
Strain Over Time
Strain (Foster et al.)
Each bar represents one training day, with strain calculated from a rolling 7-day window: weekly load × monotony. Captures both volume and sameness of training. High strain with high monotony is more dangerous than high strain with varied loading. Thresholds are relative to the athlete's own average — track trends over time rather than absolute values.
Grey <80% Low · Green 80–120% Good · Yellow 120–150% Monitor · Red >150% High Risk
